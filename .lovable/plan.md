## Enhance Tennis Editorial Prompts — Deeper Film Realism + Product Dynamics

### Current gaps

After examining the 6 Tennis prompts against the best-performing activewear scenes, here are the specific weaknesses:

1. **Film grain is mentioned generically** — phrases like "fine organic grain" and "Kodak Portra" are vague AI-bait. The best scenes don't name film stocks; they describe the *visual effect* on the image (shadow lift amount, halation behavior, mid-tone desaturation level, highlight bloom character).

2. **Product-dynamic language is formulaic** — all 6 prompts repeat nearly identical product paragraphs ("compression at the waist and hips, natural stretch pull across the shoulders"). Each scene should describe product behavior *specific to that pose and camera angle* (e.g., a seated lounge shot needs fabric pooling at the lap; a close-up needs visible knit weave at 85mm scale).

3. **Missing sport-specific body mechanics** — tennis has very distinct movement vocabulary (open-stance forehand rotation, split-step knee bend, trophy-pose serve wind-up) that would make scenes feel authentic rather than generic "model on court."

4. **No anti-AI-glow directives** — the best scenes include explicit "no fake-AI fabric behavior", "no glossy digital sheen", "no plastic skin rendering" safeguards. Tennis scenes lack these.

5. **Lighting descriptions are flat** — current prompts say "golden hour" or "side light" but don't describe how light interacts with performance fabric specifically (mesh panel translucency, reflective piping behavior, compression fabric sheen vs matte zones).

### Rewrite strategy per scene

**Tennis Court Baseline** — Add split-step or open-stance forehand body mechanics. Describe how performance mesh panels catch rim light differently from solid fabric zones. Replace "Kodak Portra" with specific grading: lifted shadows at +15%, warm amber shift in highlights, desaturated greens in the court surface, subtle halation bloom around backlit edges.

**Clay Court Warm-Up** — Add torso rotation stretch or lateral lunge. Describe terracotta dust on shoes as environmental authenticity. Product dynamics: show how fabric weight shifts during lateral hip stretch, compression band behavior at waist during rotation. Film grade: warm ochre color cast with crushed blacks, no digital clarity — like a slightly underexposed analogue scan.

**Net Approach Portrait** — Add forward-momentum body language (weight on front foot, arm mid-swing follow-through). Close-up product dynamics: visible knit structure of the collar, natural pilling zones at high-friction areas, micro-texture of performance fabric weave at 85mm macro scale. Film grade: controlled highlight clipping on skin (not HDR-recovered), organic transition from sharp focus to bokeh.

**Tennis Club Lounge** — Add post-match body language (towel on shoulder, relaxed lean, one leg crossed). Seated product dynamics: fabric pooling at the lap crease, waistband riding up naturally, logo position shifting from seated compression. Film grade: vintage warm cast like an overexposed Kodak Gold 200, soft halation on window highlights.

**Racket & Gear Flat Lay** — Add micro-detail: visible fabric grain at close range, natural shadow gradient in fold valleys, accurate hardware reflection (zipper teeth, grommet finish). Film grade: even studio light with subtle warm color cast, no harsh digital edges on shadows.

**Stadium Court Hero** — Add championship body language (confident athletic stance, slight forward lean, arms naturally at sides with purpose). Full-body product dynamics at distance: how seam lines read at full-body scale, compression gradient visible from waist to hem, fabric silhouette edge sharpness against stadium bokeh. Film grade: high-contrast sports broadcast aesthetic with crushed deep shadows, controlled grain in dark areas, blue-teal color separation in the background.
